The OpenTF Manifesto

Terraform was open-sourced in 2014 under an MPL license. Over the next 9 years, it built up a community that included thousands of users, contributors, customers, certified practitioners, vendors, and an ecosystem of open-source modules, plugins, libraries, and extensions.

On August 10th, 2023, HashiCorp switched Terraform to a non-open-source BSL license with no advance notice to the hundreds of unpaid individuals who voluntarily contributed to the success of Terraform and its ecosystem. This change threatens the entire Terraform community and ecosystem.

Our concern: the BSL license is a poison pill for Terraform.

Overnight, tens of thousands of businesses, ranging from one-person shops to the Fortune 500, woke up to a new reality where the underpinnings of their infrastructure suddenly became a potential legal risk because the legal terms in the BSL license are vague.

Now, every company, vendor, and developer using Terraform must wonder whether what they do could be construed as competitive with HashiCorp. Even those that might be in the clear now, may not be clear in the future. How can you trust that your usage won't violate the license terms in the future? What if your product changes? What if HashiCorp's product changes? What if HashiCorp changes its interpretation of "competitive?" What if they change the license again?

As a result of this license change, everything that uses Terraform is on shaky ground.

It is clear that under the new license, the thriving Terraform ecosystem will dwindle and wither. Developers will be less likely to want to learn Terraform, pick it for their projects, and contribute to its ecosystem. Companies will be less likely to use Terraform to manage their infrastructure and, instead, will pick truly open-source alternatives without licensing uncertainty. Existing Terraform codebases will turn into liabilities, independent tooling will disappear, and the Terraform community will fracture and vanish.

This sort of change not only fractures the Terraform community but it also harms all similar open-source projects. Imagine if the creators of Linux or Kubernetes suddenly switched to a non-open-source license that only permitted non-competitive usage. Would you still risk running your production software using those tools?

Now, every company and developer will think twice before adopting and investing in an open-source project in case its creator suddenly decides to change the license.

That's why the building blocks of the modern Internet, such as Linux and Kubernetes, are handed over to impartial bodies (the Linux Foundation and the Cloud Native Computing Foundation, respectively) who can ensure that those building blocks can form solid and predictable underpinnings for our industry.

Our goal: ensure Terraform is handed over to an open-source foundation.

In particular, we want to create a foundation for Terraform that is:

  • Truly open source - under a well-known and widely-accepted license that companies can trust, that won't suddenly change in the future, and isn't subject to the whims of a single vendor
  • Community-driven - so that the community governs the project for itself, where pull requests are regularly reviewed and accepted on their merit
  • Impartial - so that valuable features and fixes are accepted based on their value to the community, regardless of their impact on any particular vendor
  • Layered and modular - with a programmer-friendly project structure to encourage building on top, enabling a new vibrant ecosystem of tools and integrations
  • Backward-compatible - so that the existing code can drive value for years to come

Our request to HashiCorp: donate Terraform to this foundation.

We ask HashiCorp to do the right thing by the community: instead of going forward with the BSL license change, donate Terraform to this foundation, and keep it under a genuinely open-source license. That way, instead of fracturing the community, we'll end up with a single, impartial, reliable home for Terraform. There, the whole community can unite to keep building this amazing ecosystem.

Our fallback plan: fork Terraform.

If HashiCorp is unwilling to donate Terraform to the foundation, we propose to fork Terraform and maintain the fork in the foundation. Supporting a fork will take time, skill, effort, and coordination. To this end, this pledge will pool our resources and build a more open, inclusive future for Terraform.
